About the coalition

Families deserve coordinated care, not fragmented services.

Roughly one in four working families across our region can't afford the basics, and most are working full-time. For decades, Charlottesville's nonprofit ecosystem is resource rich but uncoordinated. A family in need will touch multiple organizations, repeating their story to each, and still fall through the cracks with regards to efficient service delivery.

PFS exists to change that. City of Promise, United Way of Greater Charlottesville, ReadyKids, University of Virginia Center for Community Partnerships, and Network2Work have worked diligently to formalize how they share intake, referrals, and outcomes.

Families tell their story once. Every referral is tracked, recorded, and followed through. Each family encounters one constellation of care, not five agencies.

Our work is grounded in mutual aid: each member organization brings its specific strengths, and together we cover the full continuum of support a family might need across a lifetime, from early childhood through college access and beyond. Truly, cradle-to-career.

How it works

A family tells their story once

A single shared intake means no family repeats their circumstances to five separate agencies.

2

Referrals move between partners

Each referral is tracked, recorded, and followed through — handed off between member organizations, not dropped.

3

Outcomes are shared and measured

The coalition measures what happens after the referral, so support continues across a family's whole journey.
