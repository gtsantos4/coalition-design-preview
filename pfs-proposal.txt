Pathways to Family Success Pilot Proposal Trailblazer Elementary | Partnership with Charlottesville 
City Schools A coordinated afterschool model to improve literacy, attendance, and family success 
=== slide2.xml ===
The Challenge Charlottesville students face persistent and widening disparities in chronic 
absenteeism and early literacy proficiency: 48-point gap in reading proficiency between Black and 
white students At Trailblazer, only 30% of Black students meet reading benchmarks vs. 89% of white 
students 14.2% of students are chronically absent (over 30% of Black students) Approximately 27% of 
local families cannot meet basic needs These challenges impact attendance, engagement, and 
long-term academic success. 
=== slide3.xml ===
Why This Matters Early academic gaps have long-term consequences: Students not reading at grade 
level by 3rd grade are more likely to struggle throughout school Chronic absenteeism in early 
grades predicts lower academic outcomes Family instability directly impacts student performance 
Education is a social issue and schools cannot solve these challenges alone—but they are central to 
the solution. 
=== slide4.xml ===
The Opportunity Charlottesville has strong community resources—but they are not coordinated. 
Families navigate multiple systems independently Services operate in parallel rather than together 
Existing supports are underutilized due to fragmentation We have the resources. We need efficiency 
and alignment. 
=== slide5.xml ===
The Solution Pathways to Family Success (PFS) is a coordinated, school-connected model that: Aligns 
academic, behavioral health, and family supports Uses integrated technology to connect students and 
families to the right resources at the right time Uses shared data to improve coordination and 
outcomes This model is grounded in a two-generation approach —supporting both students and their 
families. 
=== slide6.xml ===
Pilot Overview Trailblazer Elementary Afterschool Pilot Up to 50 students (K–5) Monday–Friday, 
2:30–5:30 PM Academic tutoring + enrichment programming Family and economic support services This 
pilot is designed to test a coordinated, community-based afterschool model. 
=== slide7.xml ===
The Ask We are kindly requesting Charlottesville City Schools’ partnership to: Approve use of 
Trailblazer Elementary for afterschool pilot Allow coordinated service delivery by coalition 
partners and non-profit practitioners in Charlottesville 
=== slide8.xml ===
What CCS Gains: This partnership supports core school priorities: Improved literacy outcomes 
through structured tutoring Reduced absenteeism through coordinated family support Increased 
student engagement and safe and productive enrichment opportunities Streamlined coordination with 
trusted community partners Access to evaluation and continuous improvement support 
=== slide9.xml ===
What CCS Does Not Need To Do This model is designed to minimize burden on CCS: No requirement to 
hire or manage additional staff No financial contribution required for pilot implementation No 
responsibility for program operations or coordination No duplication of existing school efforts 
Coalition partners lead implementation and coordination. 
=== slide10.xml ===
How it Works Coordinated support model Student &amp; Family → Intake → Services → Shared Data → 
Outcomes Intake through trusted partner organizations or school referrals Coordinated service 
delivery across partners Shared data platform ( SmartSheets ) tracks engagement and outcomes 
Continuous improvement informed by evaluation 
=== slide11.xml ===
Program Model Daily Structure 2:30–3:30 PM Academic tutoring (literacy-focused, small group) 
3:30–5:30 PM Enrichment programming + youth development Examples include: Arts and music STEM and 
hands-on learning Physical activity and wellness Social-emotional learning 
=== slide12.xml ===
Family Support Model PFS supports the whole family: Behavioral health services for students and 
caregivers Parent workforce and economic mobility support Early childhood connections for younger 
siblings Barrier removal (transportation, childcare, etc.) When families are stable, students 
succeed. 
=== slide13.xml ===
Partner Roles Coordinated, complementary roles across organizations: City of Promise: Program 
coordination, staffing, tutoring architecture, family engagement ReadyKids : Behavioral health and 
family well-being services Network2Work: Parent workforce and economic mobility pathways United 
Way: Data infrastructure ( SmartSheets ), fiscal backbone, childcare connections UVA Center for 
Community Partnerships: Evaluation and continuous improvement 
=== slide14.xml ===
Data &amp; Coordination PFS uses a shared data system to improve outcomes: SmartSheets : Tracks 
student and family engagement across partners Network2Work platform: Supports adult workforce 
pathways Consent-based data sharing ensures privacy and ethical use Goal: Reduce duplication, 
improve coordination, and ensure families receive support. 
=== slide15.xml ===
Measuring Success Pilot success will be measured by: Increased literacy proficiency among 
participating students Reduced chronic absenteeism Increased family engagement Improved parent 
employment and stability outcomes Effective coordination across partner organizations
